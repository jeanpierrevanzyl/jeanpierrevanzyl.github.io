About

Hello traveller, welcome to my personal portfolio.
I'm Jean-Pierre, a Stellenbosch University Computer Science student. I'm currently doing my Masters degree in Computational Intelligence under the supervision of Professor A.P. Engelbrecht.
For the past few years that I've been studying here, I've stayed in Majuba Men's Residence and had the privilege to serve on the leadership of the House. My experiences on the House Committe, as Primarius and as Vice-Primarius have taught me a lot about myself and made me a more rounded person. These experiences have taught me how to lead, manage my time, make sacrifices and to be part of something bigger than myself.
Research has become my passion and I aim to continue making contributions to the CI community. Today we search for new applications and adaptations of Set-Based Particle Swarm Optimization algorithms, or develop new exloration mechanisms for dynamic environments; but fundamentally, it's the pursuit of knowledge that has fascinated mankind for millennia.

I like open-source, but like money more. But I donate to Wikipedia so I guess that's a start.
The only social media I have is Linkedin, for a few reasons. Most social media platforms are designed to subconsiously addict you to their product, I don't appriciate that. Many of these tech companies follow the privacy laws (which can't keep up with innovation to start with) just enough so that their actions aren't classified as spying; but they have no respect for your privacy.
Removing social media from you life can be described as liberating, and certainly makes you more productive. A majorly overlooked aspect of our 21st centuary lives is our relationship with our smartphones. Make sure you use your smartphone, not that it controls you.
